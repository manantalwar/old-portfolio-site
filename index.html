ABOUT ME

I am a graduate student in Computer Science at the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. A proficient computer programmer, data scientist, and visionary, I am driven by my passion for the field of computation and the countless possibilities that lie ahead for leveraging my skills to create a more equitable world.

My primary interest lies at the dynamic intersection of social and computer sciences. I am fascinated by the study of social and behavioral relationships and their interactions with technology through scalable computational techniques and their consequences in the real world. I am also interested in the applications of discrete and applied mathematics to computer and information sciences.

My research focuses on the application of machine learning to solve real world problems. My most recent project focused on conceptualizing a tool for assisting parents in protecting their children from technology-driven exploitation by developing a pipeline to aggregate and classify potentially harmful app reviews. My other research project include developing an indictor for Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) and an analaysis of the state-of-the-art machine learning tools for social network analysis. I am also interested in computer science education. My recent research in this domain has focused on the applications of the Process Oriented Guided Inquiry Learning (POGIL) framework to Computer Science I classrooms. I have also taken up several teaching roles at UMass Amherst, teaching both programming as well as theoretical computer science.

On the professional front, I am a self-confident, dedicated, and diplomatic individual. I have extensive experience not only in building and managing teams but also in taking them to great heights. I am a creative individual who likes to think out of the box. I possess exceptional problem-solving and analytical abilities. I am a visionary and an impactful orator with a goal-oriented mindset.
